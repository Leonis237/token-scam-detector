The Strategy Setup

I designed a Seasonal Regime EMA Crossover strategy on the Daily timeframe with three core components:

  • Defensive Period (May 1 – Oct 31): 50% position size, Short-only via EMA20/EMA50 crossover — this is the literal "Sell in May" interpretation.
  • Aggressive Period (Nov 1 – Apr 30): 100% position size, Long & Short via EMA crossovers — capturing the "buy in October" rebound.
  • Filters: EMA200 macro trend filter + ADX(14) > 25 to avoid choppy, low-conviction markets.

Core Insights

Three key takeaways emerged from this experiment:

  • BTC is strongly Long-biased on the Daily timeframe. Forcing shorts during any season is effectively fighting the macro trend. Even in the "weak" summer months, BTC's underlying drift is upward — the shorts in V8 got caught in exactly this trap.
  • Defensive Mode is the real lifesaver. Reducing position size to 50% during the summer months manages risk effectively without going full bear. You don't need to short the market — you just need to be more selective and use less leverage when conditions are historically weaker.
  • Minara's real value is ultra-fast iteration. In minutes, I pivoted from a failing hypothesis (-1.41%) to one with a clear edge (+1.98%). Without the Optimization engine, each round would have taken hours of manual tuning. The speed of iteration is the edge.
